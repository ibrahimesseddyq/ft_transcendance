## Long-Term Thinking

We hire for long-term impact, not short-term task completion.

Our goal is to build teams that:
- Grow with the company
- Adapt to new challenges
- Contribute sustainably over time

We evaluate candidates based on their potential for long-term contribution rather than immediate narrow skill matching.

## Skills and Potential Balance

We consider both:

- Current technical competence
- Learning ability and growth potential

Strong fundamentals, problem-solving ability, and adaptability are valued as much as specific framework experience.

We believe skills can be developed when the right mindset exists.

## Structured and Fair Evaluation

Hiring decisions are based on structured evaluation criteria.

We avoid:
- Random judgment
- Personal bias
- Non-job-related factors

Candidates are assessed against predefined criteria relevant to the role.

Consistency across candidates is a priority.

## Merit-Based Selection

Selection decisions are based solely on:

- Technical ability
- Problem-solving skills
- Communication clarity
- Role alignment
- Professional conduct

Personal background, identity, or unrelated characteristics do not influence decisions.

## Transparency in Process

We aim to provide:

- Clear role descriptions
- Clear expectations
- Honest communication
- Feedback when possible

Candidates should understand what is being evaluated and why.

## Problem-Solving Orientation

We value candidates who:

- Break down complex problems
- Ask clarifying questions
- Think logically
- Communicate reasoning clearly

The ability to approach unfamiliar problems is often more important than memorized knowledge.

## Ownership Mindset

We look for individuals who demonstrate:

- Responsibility for their work
- Accountability for mistakes
- Initiative in solving issues
- Commitment to quality

We prefer proactive contributors over passive executors.

## Communication & Collaboration

Technical skill alone is not sufficient.

We evaluate:

- Clarity of explanation
- Openness to feedback
- Respectful discussion
- Ability to articulate trade-offs

Engineering is collaborative by nature.

## Ethical and Professional Standards

Professional integrity is non-negotiable.

We expect:

- Honest representation of skills
- Respectful communication
- Ethical behavior during interviews
- No plagiarism or unauthorized assistance during tests

Violations may result in disqualification.

## Diversity & Equal Opportunity

We are committed to fair and inclusive hiring practices.

We aim to create equal opportunity for all candidates regardless of:

- Gender
- Nationality
- Religion
- Disability
- Background

Hiring decisions are made strictly on job-relevant criteria.

## Constructive Rejection Philosophy

Rejection does not imply lack of capability.

Reasons for rejection may include:

- Skill mismatch for current needs
- Experience level misalignment
- Better alignment of another candidate

Candidates are encouraged to reapply when growth has occurred.

## Continuous Improvement of Hiring Process

We regularly evaluate and refine our hiring approach to:

- Reduce bias
- Improve fairness
- Increase clarity
- Enhance candidate experience

Hiring is treated as an evolving system, not a static procedure.

## Alignment Over Perfection

We do not seek perfect resumes.

We seek alignment between:

- Candidate strengths
- Role requirements
- Team needs
- Company direction

Authenticity is valued more than polished presentation.

## Respect for Candidate Time

We strive to:

- Keep interviews structured
- Avoid unnecessary rounds
- Communicate decisions in reasonable timeframes

Candidate time is respected as much as company time.

## Integrity in Decision-Making

All hiring decisions are:

- Reviewed carefully
- Based on documented criteria
- Made collaboratively when required

We avoid arbitrary or impulsive decisions.
